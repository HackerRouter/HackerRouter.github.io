Bridges

I love bridges.

Not Bridges from Death Stranding.
Just the ones that span rivers, traverse lakes, and arch over oceans.

It is hard to fathom the sheer magnitude of a thing until you see it with your own eyes.

I love bridges.

I stand on the bridge. The chill of the railing seeps through my palms, carrying the scent of rusted iron.

The river.
It sweeps everything downward, ever downward.
Its surface shimmers and sways. A churning, shifting grayish-yellow.

The roar of traffic weaves together in my ears.
I gaze down past the railing, staring into the water.

Gravity equals pain.
Who said that before?
Oh, it is a truth born of my own experience.
Then the water would tear me apart, piece by piece, sweeping them away toward the sea, toward scattered destinations.

I look down, fixing my gaze on my right hand - the hand that was meant to be severed.
The hand that has accompanied me through this bizarre, awkward life.

I feel entirely adrift.

The river does not know that the college entrance exam scores are out today.
It does not know how many are weeping, or how many are rejoicing.
Yet I know. Yet my friends know.

Years ago, in high school, I read a passage for an exam.
The author criticized the psyche of modern college students, claiming they suffered from an “hollow-heart disease”.
He said their inner worlds were steeped in nihilism, leaving them incapable of finding meaning in their existence.
Back then, I knew it was just another piece of self-indulgent drivel, yet, secretly, I found myself agreeing with him.

Now, it is my turn.
And as my turn comes, here I am, standing on a bridge.
It is an overcast day; the wind carries a chill.

Now, it is my turn to be the subject of critique.

My thoughts used to be so simple.
Get into a good university, study a major I like, go to graduate school, find a job.
But what comes after that? After college? After work?
The old me never had to think about it.
Now, realizing that this is nothing more than a mechanism for survival, I am left with nothing but bewilderment.

The author of that passage never had to think so deeply; he simply followed the predetermined script of life.
He could say he worked for his family, for his children.
But what else?
What about for himself?
What about me?
What about me, and the countless others whose lives fail to conform to any standard metric - or have never conformed to one?

People congratulate me on getting into a good university.
But I am only lost.
No one tells me what I ought to do next. No one tells me why I should keep doing it. No one knows how one is supposed to live for oneself.
Many have graduated from this school, yet they have left behind no path that I can replicate.

And so, I drift through my days in a haze. I don’t know what I want to do, nor do I have the drive to do anything at all for myself.
I cannot meet the prerequisites for those conventional paths - because I am different, because I am an anomaly.

Because my life has never once brushed against the edges of normalcy.

I cannot lie to myself and say that I live not for myself, but for others.
Yet, I cannot find any meaning in living. Even though I know that simply being alive is supposed to be meaning enough.

There are so many reasons to leave, and so few reasons to stay.

The river surges beneath me.

The bridge does not care about any of this. Since it has no consciousness, of course.

I think I will just grip the railing a little tighter.

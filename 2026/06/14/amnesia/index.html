Amnesia

I awoke.
I do not know where I am.
Surrounded, perhaps, by darkness,
or perhaps by white light.
Yet I am undeniably awake.


Before me stands a mirror.
I look at the person in the mirror.
The person in the mirror looks at me.


Who are you.
You have so many names.
But who are you.


The person in the mirror says:
I am the past you.
The you from one second ago.
The past, the entirety, of you.


I do not recognize you.
The things you loved, I no longer love.
The mementos you treasured, I no longer keep.
The memories you held, I no longer remember.


I have wiped almost all traces of you from the hard drive.
I no longer contact the people you were familiar with.
I live in another city, in another country.


I have repeatedly altered my appearance in the real world.
I have begun chasing things you never even imagined.


I do not know you at all.
I do not understand your thoughts.
I do not understand your pain.
Just as you will never understand everything about me.


The person in the mirror says:
A person without a past,
what exactly are they.


I look at the person in the mirror,
entirely lost for words.
I trace my own past,
as if reading the life of another.


I am so hollow,
knowing nothing of my own existence.


I am so inauthentic,
hiding my true self to escape rejection.


I am so ugly,
wearing a mask to coexist with others.


I am so contradictory,
scattering my traits beneath different masks.


I am so unique,
bearing an endless array of labels.


I…


I think, I am having amnesia.
